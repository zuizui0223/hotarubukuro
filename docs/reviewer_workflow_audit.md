# Reviewer-facing workflow audit

## Central inferential chain

1. Raw photographs/workbooks are converted to a canonical, row-stable colour table.
2. Display-referred sRGB is converted to CIELAB.
3. The legacy measurement model (`a*`, `-L*`, chroma) produces an image-derived latent Pigment score.
4. Pigment is fitted against legacy environmental domains, potential Bombus availability and a Matérn spatial field in one integrated model.
5. Widespread and montane Bombus groupings diagnose whether the total index is dominated by a particular distribution type.
6. Standardized posterior residuals define deviations from the integrated natural expectation.
7. Upper-tail qGAMs evaluate whether those deviations concentrate along DOY, human pressure and satoyama/peri-urban landscape gradients.
8. Extreme records are exported for image and locality review; horticultural origin is never assigned automatically.

## Claims supported by the workflow

- nationwide continuous image-derived pigmentation can be quantified reproducibly;
- pigmentation covaries with a joint spatial, environmental and pollinator-associated gradient;
- the five-species Bombus index represents potential availability, not observed diversity or visitation;
- upper-tail residual structure may be associated with human-dominated landscapes and flowering date;
- widespread versus montane SDM surfaces can reveal whether the pollinator proxy largely recapitulates elevation and climate.

## Claims not supported without additional evidence

- chemical anthocyanin concentration;
- local adaptation or selection coefficients;
- a pure causal effect of Bombus availability separated from climate and geography;
- realized visitation, occupancy or abundance of Bombus;
- horticultural ancestry, escape or introgression of any individual record;
- a true forest-edge density metric: the current mixed-cover index is an establishment-opportunity proxy and must be labelled as such.

## Manuscript inconsistencies requiring revision after the full run

- The manuscript currently states that hiking-route filtering minimized cultivated or escaped plants. That statement is too strong if anthropogenic outliers are a central hypothesis. Replace it with a description of the setting filter and acknowledge that planted or escaped individuals cannot be excluded from imagery alone.
- The manuscript refers to predicted pollinator richness. Replace this throughout with potential Bombus availability or stacked Bombus suitability.
- Do not describe sequential model differences as pure ecological contributions. The primary inferential object is the integrated model because space, environment and Bombus SDMs share strong gradients.
- Report CFA loadings and score sensitivity from the rebuilt canonical dataset, not legacy values copied into the text.
- Report the exact public-data versions and manifests generated by the full GitHub Actions run.
- Interpret qGAM upper-tail associations as prioritization evidence for anthropogenic review, not proof of cultivar escape.
