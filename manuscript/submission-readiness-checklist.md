# Submission-readiness checklist

The analytical narrative and numerical results are locked to the current
publication registry. The following author- or institution-controlled items
must be confirmed before the manuscript is uploaded.

## Author metadata

- exact department, graduate school, postal address, and country;
- corresponding-author email and ORCID;
- final author list, order, and CRediT roles;
- conflict-of-interest declaration; and
- funding numbers, institutional support, and acknowledgements.

The generated draft currently uses “Kyoto University, Kyoto, Japan” and a
single-author CRediT statement as working metadata. These are not inferred
beyond the information available in the project.

## Data and platform governance

- confirm the terms, permissions, attribution, privacy handling, and ethical
  basis for using YAMAP activity records and derived photograph measurements;
- confirm which source identifiers and reviewed coordinates may be released;
- do not redistribute original third-party photographs automatically;
- document why one curated record lacks the retained `yamap_activity`
  provenance label even though it was identified through YAMAP; and
- archive the final data and code release in a DOI-issuing repository and
  replace the provisional Data Accessibility Statement with its DOI.

## Figures and submission files

- regenerate the final figures from the locked stage outputs;
- cite every table and figure in numerical order in the main text;
- prepare journal-compliant figure files and alt text;
- check that the final title page and declarations match the submission
  system; and
- verify the final Word or PDF rendering in Microsoft Word before upload.

## Interpretation gates

- CIELAB a* is visible image colour, not anthocyanin concentration or a
  bumblebee receptor contrast;
- ENMeval output is relative habitat suitability, not abundance, richness,
  visitation, or pollination effectiveness;
- the local *Bombus* result is turnover correspondence, not
  pollinator-mediated selection;
- the route-restricted YAMAP frame can attenuate human-context contrasts and
  confound accessibility with exposure; and
- the human-context analyses do not establish horticultural introduction,
  escape, introgression, or genetic pollution.
